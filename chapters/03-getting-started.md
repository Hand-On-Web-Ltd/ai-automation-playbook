# Chapter 3: Getting Started

So you want to automate something. Here's how to actually do it without wasting time or money.

## Step 1: Pick One Thing

Don't try to automate everything at once. Pick the one process that:

- Takes up the most time
- Is the most repetitive
- Annoys your team the most
- Follows a fairly predictable pattern

Good first projects:
- Answering FAQs on your website
- Booking appointments by phone or chat
- Sorting incoming enquiries
- Sending follow-up messages

Bad first projects:
- Anything that requires complex judgment
- Processes that change every week
- Things where mistakes would be really costly

## Step 2: Map Out the Process

Before you build anything, write down exactly how the process works today:

1. What triggers it? (customer calls, email arrives, form submitted)
2. What steps happen? (read message, check calendar, send reply)
3. What decisions get made? (is this urgent? does the time slot work?)
4. Where does the info end up? (CRM, calendar, spreadsheet)

This doesn't need to be fancy. A bullet list on a page is fine. The point is to understand what you're automating before you start.

## Step 3: Choose Your Approach

There are three main paths:

### DIY with No-Code Tools
**Tools:** Make.com, Zapier, n8n, Voiceflow
**Good for:** Simple automations, connecting existing apps
**Cost:** £0-100/month
**Skill needed:** You can figure these out in a weekend

### Pre-Built Solutions
**What:** Off-the-shelf AI chatbots, scheduling tools, email tools
**Good for:** Standard use cases where an existing product fits
**Cost:** £20-200/month
**Skill needed:** Basic setup and configuration

### Custom Build
**What:** A developer (or agency like us) builds exactly what you need
**Good for:** Anything specific to your business, complex workflows, voice AI
**Cost:** £1,000-10,000+ depending on complexity
**Skill needed:** None — that's why you're hiring someone

## Step 4: Start Small, Test, Expand

Whatever you build:

1. **Start with a pilot** — run it alongside your existing process for a week or two
2. **Check the results** — is it actually saving time? Are customers happy? Any weird errors?
3. **Tweak it** — fix the edge cases that come up in real use
4. **Roll it out** — once it's working, make it the default
5. **Expand** — move on to the next process

## Step 5: Get Your Team On Board

The biggest blocker isn't usually the technology — it's people worried about change. Be upfront:

- "This handles the boring stuff so you can focus on [interesting thing]"
- "Nobody's getting replaced — we're freeing up your time"
- Show them the results early so they see the benefit

## Common Mistakes

- **Trying to automate everything at once** — pick one thing, nail it, then move on
- **Not mapping the process first** — you'll build the wrong thing
- **Expecting perfection from day one** — AI gets better with tuning
- **Ignoring edge cases** — plan for the weird situations, not just the happy path
- **Not measuring results** — track time saved, customer satisfaction, error rates
